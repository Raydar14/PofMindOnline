Our Approach

Forms of therapy we practice

Therapy isn't one-size-fits-all. We draw from several evidence-based approaches and tailor them to your goals, your pace, and your story.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Identify the thought patterns that fuel anxiety, depression, and anger, then learn practical skills to challenge and change them. Structured, goal-oriented, and backed by decades of research.

Humanistic & Person-Centered

You are the expert on your own life. We provide a warm, non-judgmental space to explore your emotions, desires, and goals, and to grow toward the person you want to be.

Mindfulness-Informed Therapy

Learn to observe thoughts and feelings without being swept away by them. Mindfulness skills help calm the body's stress response and build steadiness in daily life.

Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT)

Stop fighting difficult thoughts and start living by your values. ACT builds psychological flexibility so discomfort no longer decides what you do next.

Psychodynamic Therapy

Understand how earlier experiences and relationship patterns shape how you feel and connect today. Insight that creates room for deeper, lasting change.

Couples & Relational Work

Systemic approaches that treat the relationship itself as the client: improving communication, rebuilding trust, and helping each partner feel heard and cared for.

Also available: sport and performance psychology with Dr. Hulsey for athletes, performers, and high achievers, blending evidence-based therapy with mental-performance training.

Questions

Online therapy, answered

Is online therapy as effective as in-person?

For most concerns, including anxiety, depression, and relationship issues, research consistently finds teletherapy comparable in effectiveness to in-person care. Many clients find it easier to open up from the comfort of their own space, and consistency improves when sessions fit your schedule.

What is PSYPACT, and why does it matter?

PSYPACT is an interstate compact that allows licensed psychologists with an Authority to Practice Interjurisdictional Telepsychology (APIT) to see clients located in any participating state. It means you can keep the same therapist if you move, travel, or attend school in another PSYPACT state. No starting over.

What do I need for a session?

A phone, tablet, or computer with a camera; a reliable internet connection; and a private, quiet space. We'll send you a secure video link; there's nothing to install and no account to create.

Do you accept insurance?

We do not accept insurance. We can provide a superbill you may submit to your insurer for possible out-of-network reimbursement. Rates are listed transparently above so there are no surprises.

Can we do couples therapy if we're in different locations?

Yes. As long as each of you is located in a PSYPACT state during the session, you can join from separate places. It's a popular option for long-distance couples and busy schedules.

What if I'm in Tallahassee and prefer in-person?

Our Midtown Tallahassee office is open. Visit therapytallahassee.com for in-person therapy, plus services like biofeedback, sports psychology, and ketamine-assisted therapy.

Is online therapy right for a crisis?

Online therapy is not a substitute for emergency care. If you're in crisis or having thoughts of harming yourself, call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or go to your nearest emergency room.
